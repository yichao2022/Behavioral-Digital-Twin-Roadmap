# The Behavioral Digital Twin (BDT) Framework: An Executive Summary
### Prepared by Yichao (Cary) Jin | PhD Candidate, UT Dallas

## 1. Concept: From Static Data to Generative Agents
The Behavioral Digital Twin (BDT) framework represents a shift from traditional aggregate-level modeling to individual-level generative simulation. By integrating economic choice theory with machine learning, the BDT creates "digital shadows" of specific populations that can "experience" policy or clinical changes in a risk-free, simulated environment.

## 2. Core Logic: The "Decision Drift" Model
Applying structural behavioral economics and neuro-computational models, the BDT quantifies the transition from intent to action. We focus on three critical behavioral parameters:
- **Cognitive Friction ($\kappa$):** The metabolic and psychological cost of processing complex clinical information.
- **Present Bias ($\beta$):** The tendency to prioritize immediate comfort (e.g., avoiding an invasive test) over long-term survival.
- **Trust Depreciation ($\delta$):** The decay of institutional or clinical trust over time, leading to adherence failures.

These parameters allow us to model **"Patient Decision Drift"**—the structural decay of medical intent into a state of indefinite delay.

## 3. Potential Synergies with UCSF Decision Lab
Within the context of decision neuroscience and aging, the BDT framework provides:

- Predictive Vulnerability Modeling: Quantifying how neuro-cognitive decline alters an individual's decision-making architecture, leading to increased risk of financial exploitation.
- Intervention Sandboxing: Testing the efficacy of clinical or social interventions in a simulated environment before deploying them to vulnerable populations.
- Precision Behavioral Insights: Moving beyond aggregate means to identify which specific "decision environments" are most protective for individuals with specific neurological profiles.

## 4. Technical Foundations
- Agent-Based Modeling (ABM) for complex social interactions.
- Causal inference frameworks for robust policy forecasting.
- NLP and Social Media integration for real-time behavioral signal detection.
